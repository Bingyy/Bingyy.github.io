6. clock in 打卡上班

例句：Researchers found that employees’ moods when they clocked in tended to affect how they felt the rest of the day.

造句：I could clock in by 10 am and clock out by 5 pm today.

7. The fact that 事实上

例句：The fact that we tend to do this twice a day, five days a week, makes it unsurprising that its effect would build up over time and make us less and less happy.

造句： The fact that people incline to have sweet foods as much as possible originates in human’s evolution from which the more sugar we can get the more chances to survive.

8. acclimate to 使适应环境

例句：While many voluntary conditions don’t affect our happiness in the long term because we acclimate to them, people never get accustomed to their daily slog to work because sometimes the traffic is awful and sometimes it’s not.

造句： It is not that hard to acclimate to a new place for human beings at all. I found this out when I moved around several cities in the past years. Always, it only takes several days to settle down and then it is time to enjoy the moment.

9. compensate for sth by doing sth 通过做某事来弥补

例句：We tend to try to compensate for this by having a bigger house or a better job.

造句：Please do let the idea go which you can compensate for something by doing something else. Be prepared to do it well at first while you get the chance.

10. make up for 补偿，弥补

例句：Two Swiss economists who studied the effect of commuting on happiness found that such factors could not make up for the misery created by a long commute.

造句：Since I cannot undo the past I am considering a new plan to make up for you and if you accept the items please sign the contract.
